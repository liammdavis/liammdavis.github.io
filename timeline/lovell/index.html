--- layout: info title: Lovell Health House ---

The Lovell House was designed by Richard Neutra, and for its innovative approach to modern residential construction, it is part of what put him on the map. Neutra looked to other architects, specifically Le Courbusier and Frank Lloyd Wright, and with the Lovell House charted a course between them. Specifically Corbusier's streamlined aesthetic, and Wright’s planar devices. The Lovell House is also likely the first steel framed residential house in the United States. A technique that was used in Skyscrapers beginning in the late 1800’s, and that Neutra became familiar with while working in Chicago and New York. The home also employed one of the first uses of gunite, where a concrete mixture was sprayed onto the facade of the building. AD
