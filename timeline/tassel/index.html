--- layout: info title: Hôtel Tassel ---

This home is an unfortunately beautiful monument to Belgian atrocities in the Congo. Victor Horta’s Hotel Tassel was commissioned by Émile Tassel and it is a precursor to the Art Nouveau movement. It was a culmination of Horta’s big ideas, including exposing the iron structure of the building, integrating the decoration into the structure, fluidity in the building, natural light, and a central greenhouse. Additionally it was Horta’s intent that the home be a reflection of the occupant. While construction was completed in 1894, Horta spent several more years working on furniture for the home. IPA
