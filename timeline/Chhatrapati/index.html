Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus, originally known as Victoria Terminus, was the largest British colonial building in India at the time of its construction. It was built in the aftermath of an 1857 revolt that occurred across British occupied India, and its gothic revival styling was intended to re-establish a sense of British superiority in their colony. Its design is reminiscent of St. Pancras Station in London, however with significant influence from the country it was built in, since it was constructed in India by Indian people for that climate and with techniques and materials native to the region.
